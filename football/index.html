Introduction

Steve and Nick are lifelong Man City fans, going to a few matches a season. When it was announced that City had signed up for the Super League (in 2021), we were... disappointed. We remain City fans. However, we decided that when we wanted to watch a match, we would also consider matches at our regional clubs.

Our goal was to incorporate the elements we enjoyed when we went to see a City match. We would go by public transport, find a good chippy nearest to the ground and, with a nod to the modern age, have a veg or vegan pie at half time, as well as a pint.

Our first trip was to Hallam FC, which is a 10 minute walk from where we live, missing out the chippy and pie.

2024/25

Tue 8 May 2025, Hallam

Worksop Town 2 - 1 Hallam
Sheffield & Hallamshire Senior Cup

Played at Hillsborough. 1-0 at half-time. Great equaliser from Rio Allan, but Worksop got a second. Valiant performance from Hallam given that Worksop play in a league two tiers above Hallam.
Worksop played in the seventh tier (Northern Premier League Premier Division) this season and won promotion to the sixth tier (National League North) for next season.
Hallam played in the ninth tier (Northern Counties East League Premier Division) this season and won promotion to the eighth tier (Northern Premier League Division One East) for next season.

Sat 19 April 2025, Hallam

Hallam 2 - 2 Beverley Town
Hallam win 4-3 on penalties
Northern Counties East Football League (NCEL) Premier Division Play-Offs Final

Walk to the ground. Second half performance sees Hallam cancel out two-goal deficit from first half. Late goal for Hallam disallowed. Offside. And then penalties at the Shed End. Hallam to take the first penalty. Tense. 3-3. Miss for Hallam. Then, one of the Beverley players makes a hash of a panenka. Hallam score. Hallam save the last penalty. Northern Premier League next season, the eighth tier of English football.

Tue 21 Jan 2025, Hallam

Hallam 2 - 1 Sheffield FC
Sheffield & Hallamshire Senior Cup Quarter-finals

'Rules Derby', the world's two oldest football clubs in the world's oldest derby at the world's oldest ground. Attendance: 1496. Brilliant atmosphere. Shed End in full voice. Double-pints the order of the day. Great game. Match highlights at Hallam FC website. Good writeup by Dan Hayes in The Tribune
